Creating First Emails for a New Slate

21 min

Walks through the end-to-end workflow for generating first-email drafts for a new HAVEN campaign slate using Google Sheets, Slack triggers, Lindy AI automation, and Google Apps Script.

  1. Review and select themes/events from the Themes document (e.g., PGA Championship, Suncoast Boat Show) for the campaign.
  2. Prepare the "New Drafts Creation Spreadsheet" with the correct keyword (New Themes) so the Lindy agent follows the right automation path.
  3. Copy theme data for each market (New England, Philadelphia, Tampa, Palm Beach, Arizona Southwest, Las Vegas, Hawaii) into the spreadsheet.
  4. Post the prepared rows into a Slack channel, triggering the Lindy AI workflow to auto-generate first-email drafts per market.
  5. Collect generated drafts, apply formatting via Apps Script, and verify trigger words, seasonal language, events, and feeder markets.
  6. Split drafts into separate documents per market using another Apps Script.
  7. Create updated data notes for the slate (e.g., "spring market" messaging).
  8. Review and lightly edit drafts — event wording, feeder markets, etc.
  9. Send drafts for internal review (Sophie → Usama → Web-Ray channel for testing).

In short: demonstrates how to automatically generate, organize, and prepare first email outreach drafts for multiple HAVEN markets using AI automation triggered through Slack.

PA Creation + RE Creation

18 min

Explains the process for creating PA (Previous Advertiser) email drafts and RE (follow-up) email drafts for a HAVEN campaign slate after the first email drafts are completed.

  1. Create PA drafts by copying the same market drafts (e.g., New England, Philadelphia, Tampa, Palm Beach, Arizona, Las Vegas, Hawaii) into the PA creation document.
  2. Run the PA formatting script, which adjusts the email wording to reflect that the recipient has advertised previously, adding familiarity and relationship-based language.
  3. Update specific lines manually, such as replacing the closing question with wording like: "Would you like me to share rate details or pull up your last layout?"
  4. Copy the finalized PA drafts into each market's document for use in upcoming campaigns.
  5. Generate RE (follow-up) drafts by modifying the Slack trigger keyword to "RE:", which tells the Lindy AI agent to create follow-up emails based on the existing first email themes.
  6. Paste the market links into Slack, allowing Lindy to analyze the first emails and automatically generate RE drafts for each theme.
  7. Organize and format the RE drafts using Apps Script and adjust pricing or promotional offers (e.g., BOGO deals or different price points depending on the market).
  8. Review deals and spam-filter considerations (removing bold/italic formatting).
  9. Save RE drafts in the RE creation document, then later paste them into each market's draft document before the follow-up emails are scheduled to send.

In short: shows how to create relationship-focused PA emails and automated RE follow-up emails using Lindy AI, Slack triggers, and formatting scripts, then organize them for each market before sending.

Creating Supplementary Drafts

17 min

Demonstrates the workflow for creating supplementary email drafts for a HAVEN campaign slate — similar to generating RE drafts but requiring additional manual adjustments for edition variables, market names, and seasonal language.

  1. Identify the slate markets (e.g., Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Upstate, Minnesota, Canada, Vancouver, California).
  2. Trigger the Lindy AI automation by posting draft links into the Slack draft creation channel to generate supplementary drafts.
  3. Open and organize the generated drafts by market.
  4. Adjust edition variables and market names (e.g., Homes + Lifestyles, specific city editions) to ensure the correct distribution language.
  5. Copy the drafts into a master document, labeling sections by market.
  6. Run a formatting script to separate and organize the drafts automatically.
  7. Manually update certain sections using content from the previous campaign slate to ensure consistency with existing templates.
  8. Verify trigger keywords (e.g., "spring market") and update seasonal language where needed.
  9. Update special drafts such as Under $300K first email and RE drafts.
  10. Remove bold/italic formatting to standardize the email style.
  11. Copy finalized supplementary drafts into the Data Notes documents for each market so the sales team can use them.

In short: shows the process of generating, editing, and organizing supplementary outreach email drafts across multiple markets using Lindy automation, manual template adjustments, and standardized formatting before inserting them into campaign data notes.
